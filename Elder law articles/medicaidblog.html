What Happens After Your Trademark is Published?

Medi-Cal is the Califorina Medical Assistance Program. Medicaid is called Medi-Cal in California. It is a state program that will provide health coverage to qualified, low-income California residents and seniors.

If you recieved Medi-Cal at home, any benefits paid while you were 55 years or older will be subject to Medi-Cal recovery, or who (at any age) received benefits in a nursing home, unless there is a surviving spouse or a minor, blind or disabled child. If you received Medi-Cal benefits under the age of 55, then you do not have to repay the State for what it pays out for your medical care costs.

Estate recovery is how the state recovers its costs. IN 2012-2013, 8,533 estate recovery cases were opened to try to recoup some of the health care costs the State pays on behalf of Medi-Cal beneficiaries.

Estate recovery cases are opened when deceased Medi-Cal beneficiaries received services on or after their 55th birthday, and there was some indication of assets owned at the time of death.

California's definition of 'estate' includes such assests as living trusts (as long as the beneficiary's name is still on the property at the time of death), joint tenancies, tenancies in common and life estates, although claims onn the remainder interest in life estates are limited to those that were revocable.

Amount of recovery is limited to thte amount of benefits paid or to the value of the beneficiary's estate, whichever is less. The State can only collect up to the amount that is your part of the estate. The value of the estate is also reduced by the outstanding mortgages or debts on the home, burial costs, or other documented expenses.
